Resources

The D4D Coalition recommends these resources, developed by D4D members and partners, to help your organization protect its mission and fight for democratic resilience.

  • Election Cyber Incident Communications Plan Template For Political Parties and Campaigns

    Harvard Kennedy School’s Belfer Center for Science and International Affairs set out to provide campaign, political party, and election professionals with practical guides to the most applicable cybersecurity best practices in advance of the 2018 midterm elections in the United States.

  • Information Disorder: The Essential Glossary

    A glossary featuring words, acronyms, and phrases related to information disorder.

  • El Manual Internacional de Ciberseguridad para Campañas Electorales - Edición en Español

    Una guía fácil de comprender y de alto impacto para proteger su campaña política.

  • The Cybersecurity Campaign Playbook

    A high impact, easy to understand guide to securing your political campaign.

  • Democratic Principles for an Open Internet

    An Internet openness framework for democracy advocates.

  • Cybersecurity in Elections

    A report outlining strategies to strengthen and secure election management bodies.

  • Journalism, 'Fake News' and Disinformation: A Handbook for Journalism Education and Training

    A guide for journalists navigating the increasingly murky informational environment.

  • Supporting Information Integrity and Civil Political Discourse

    As part of a global NDI effort to promote information integrity, NDI developed an awareness-raising resource for distribution to NDI staff and local partners.

  • The Cybersecurity Campaign Playbook: India Edition

    A guide to securing political campaigns in India.

  • Disinformation Campaigns and Hate Speech: Exploring the Relationship and Programming Interventions

    A framework for countering disinformation campaigns and hate speech.

  • Disinformation and Electoral Integrity: A Guidance Document for NDI Elections Programs

    NDI's programmatic approach on addressing the threat of disinformation in the electoral context.

  • Tweets that Chill: Analyzing Online Violence Against Women in Politics

    Analysis of three case studies on challenges women face when they engage in online political discourse.

  • Sorting Fact From Fiction: Nigeria's 2019 Elections

    Report on factchecking during Nigeria's 2019 elections.

  • Social Media, Disinformation and Electoral Integrity

    Responses that election management bodies and international nongovernmental organizations can take to address disinformation through social media and its threat to the integrity of elections.
